# Yale Research Design Context

## Aesthetic Anchors

Primary: Linear for clarity, density, and product polish.
Secondary: New York Review of Books for typographic seriousness in abstracts, summaries, and paper context.
Structural reference only: Are.na for adjacency and non-hierarchical browsing.

Avoid generic AI gradients, oversized SaaS heroes, decorative network graphs, university-directory nostalgia, and Stripe Press-style grandeur.

## Visual Principles

The product should feel editorial, precise, and alive. It should be dense enough for research scanning but calm enough that students trust it. Use typography, source rows, metadata, and hierarchy instead of spectacle.

## Typography

Use Inter for interface text. Use Georgia or a similar serif fallback for longer abstracts and cluster summaries. Suggested scale: 12px metadata, 14px body, 16px card titles, 20px section headings, 28px page titles, 36px homepage title/search framing.

Do not scale text with viewport width. Letter spacing should stay neutral except small uppercase metadata labels.

## Color Usage

Base: off-white app background, white surfaces, near-black text, gray metadata, pale gray borders. Use restrained semantic colors for evidence and status only.

## Yale Blue Rules

Use Yale blue as a trust accent: wordmark, links, active nav underline, focus rings, primary CTA, and thin card accent rules. Do not use Yale blue as a full-page background, gradient wash, or decorative glow.

## Spacing And Density

Use an 8px spacing grid. Keep desktop layouts compact: 24px gutters, 1300px max width, 260-280px sidebars, 16-20px card padding. Cards should use 6-8px radius, 1px borders, and very subtle hover states.

## Component Priorities

Primary components: topic cluster card, identity-confidence card, evidence/source row, and Best Next Step panel. Secondary components include search bar, paper card, citation row, filters, related topics, saved control, loading state, and empty state.

## Interaction Principles

Search should reveal interpretation, not pretend certainty. Results should explain why they matched. CTAs should appear after evidence. Hover/focus transitions should be fast and restrained, around 120-160ms.

## Metadata And Citation Treatment

Show authors, venue, year, citation count, DOI/arXiv/source links, departments, affiliations, dates, and observed/source metadata in muted 12px text. Paper abstracts and summaries should be readable, not card filler.

## Confidence Labels

Use literal labels: "Identity: Yale-confirmed," "Paper match: high," "Cluster: experimental," "Evidence: strong." Avoid vague checkmarks or badges that imply more certainty than the data supports.

## Visual Anti-Patterns

No fake personalization, decorative network graphs, giant hero sections, gradient AI glow, nested cards, huge rounded pills, blue page washes, or direct-contact CTAs without evidence context.
